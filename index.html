Chinese Medicine practitioners look at you as a whole, not just one disease or ailment. Your first appointment will take longer than a normal session, usually 90 minutes. This allows the practitioner ample time to complete a thorough health history and asses where the problem lies. Your practitioner will work with you to determine a treatment plan that is best for you and will then most likely begin treatment.

How you can prepare for your first appointment:

  • Eat at least two hours prior to treatment.
  • Bring a list of current medications, including prescriptions, over the counter, vitamins and herbal supplements.
  • Wear loose fitting clothing. Wearing clothing that is comfortable and can be rolled up is appropriate.

Friendly Reminder: Treatments will not be performed on individuals who are intoxicated.

After your initial consulation and treatment plan has been set, you will continue your plan with 1 or more follow-up treatment session. Most people find treatments to be very relaxing and may fall asleep during treatment. Again, Ellen will come up with a treatment plan to fit your individual needs.

About

Ellen received her Master of Science in Acupuncture (MSAc) from the Pacific College of Oriental Medicine in NYC. A full-time four year graduate program, she gained knowledge from the best teachers in the United States, as well as Doctors from China. Along with studying Acupucnture, Ellen is trained in Eastern nutrition, Tui Na (Chinese Massage), Cupping and Gua Sha. Ellen is a licensed acupuncturist (LAc) and board certified as a Diplomat of Oriental Medicine from the National Certification Commision for Acupuncture and Oriental Medicine (NCCAOM) in Acupucnure, Oriental Medicine, and Bioscience. She is certified by the Council of Colleges of Acupuncture and Oriental Medicine (CCAOM) in Clean Needle Techniqe and certified by the Nationl Acupuncture Detoxificaiton Association (NADA) as an Acupuncture Detoxification Specialist.
